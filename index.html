Overview

This page explores long-run U.S. macroeconomic dynamics from 1975Q1 to 2024Q1 using quarterly data: inflation (year-over-year), unemployment rate, and GDP (level + YoY growth). The goal is descriptive: highlight patterns, co-movements, and key episodes.

Interpretation note: relationships shown here are correlational and do not imply causality.

Method & limitations

Data are harmonized to quarterly frequency. Inflation is computed as YoY change of the quarterly-average CPI. Unemployment is the quarterly average of monthly unemployment rate. GDP is quarterly level, with YoY growth.

Limitations: GDP is not inflation-adjusted here (nominal). Aggregation may hide intra-quarter shocks. Long-run GDP level partly reflects price level changes.
